Per population, standardised by Pākehā rate

These figures are scaled by the per population rate for Pākehā, which allows it to be compared with with per population rates for Māori and Pacific people.

For example, Māori were 6.12 times as likely as Pākehā on a per population basis to have had handcuffs/restraints used on them by NZ Police in a reportable use of tactical options in 2014.

The Pākehā rate is always 1.00 after scaling, so as you select different tactical options you'll see that bar never changes height.

 

Per 100,000 population

These figures are per 100,000 population for the stated ethnicity. For example, 24 per 100,000 people who are Pākehā had handcuffs/restraints used on them by NZ Police in a reportable use of tactical options in 2014.

* Values have been calculated from statistics in this report: NZ Police Annual Tactical Options Research Report #3
